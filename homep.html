Frequently Asked Questions

1. Who can donate blood?

Generally, people between the ages of 18 and 65 who are in good health and weigh at least 50kg can donate blood. However, please consult a healthcare provider for individual eligibility.

2. How often can I donate blood?

You can donate whole blood once every 56 days. Other types of blood donations (like platelets) may have different eligibility criteria.

3. What happens to my blood after donation?

Your donated blood is tested, processed, and separated into components (such as red cells, plasma, and platelets). These are then given to patients in need of them.
